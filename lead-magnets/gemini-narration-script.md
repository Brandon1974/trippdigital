# Google Gemini Prompting Guide - Narration Script (2-3 minutes)

## [SLIDE 1: Title Slide]
"Welcome to The Complete Guide to Prompting Google Gemini in 2026. I'm going to show you how to get the best results from Google's powerful AI models."

## [SLIDE 2: The Core Principle]
"Here's the key insight about Gemini: A single prompt does not work equally well across all models. Each Gemini model has its own personality and rewards different prompting styles. Understanding these differences is crucial."

## [SLIDE 3: Introduce the Framework]
"Gemini uses a simple four-part framework for prompting: Persona, Task, Context, and Format.

This is Google's recommended approach, and it works brilliantly."

## [SLIDE 4: Persona - Define the Role]
"Persona: Tell Gemini what expertise or perspective it should adopt. What role should it play? Include years of experience and specific domain expertise.

Example: 'You are a senior data analyst with 10 years of experience in e-commerce metrics.'"

## [SLIDE 5: Task - Be Explicit]
"Task: State exactly what Gemini should do. Be clear and specific about your goal.

Don't say 'analyze our data.' Say 'Analyze our Q2 sales data and identify the top three drivers of customer acquisition.'"

## [SLIDE 6: Context - Provide Background]
"Context: Include all relevant information. What's your industry? What's the time period? What data do you have? What constraints exist?

The more context you provide, the better Gemini understands what you need."

## [SLIDE 7: Format - Specify Output Structure]
"Format: Tell Gemini exactly how to structure the response. Do you want bullets? Sections? A table? A specific word count?

Be explicit about the format you need."

## [SLIDE 8: Model-Specific Strategies]
"Here's where it gets interesting. Different Gemini models need different approaches.

Gemini 3.5 Flash works best with a task-chain approach. Break complex requests into clear sequential steps.

Gemini 3 standard prefers rich narrative descriptions. Provide detailed context and descriptions."

## [SLIDE 9: Real Example - Task Chain]
"For Gemini 3.5 Flash, structure like this:

Step one: Summarize the key features of our product.
Step two: Identify the main customer pain points it solves.
Step three: Create a thirty-second pitch for our target audience.

See? Clear, sequential steps."

## [SLIDE 10: Real Example - Rich Description]
"For Gemini 3 standard, be more descriptive: 'Describe a scene: A bustling farmer's market at dawn, with vendors setting up stalls, soft morning light streaming through trees, customers beginning to arrive with reusable bags. The mood is hopeful and energetic.'

Rich description works better for this model."

## [SLIDE 11: Important Setting]
"One critical thing: Keep Gemini's temperature at its default value of 1.0. Google optimized Gemini's reasoning for the default temperature. Changing it can reduce quality.

Leave it alone. It's already perfect."

## [SLIDE 12: Gemini's Multimodal Superpowers]
"Gemini in 2026 is multimodal. You can provide text, images, even video. It can analyze images, understand context from visuals, and reason across multiple types of input.

Leverage this. Include relevant images or visual context when helpful."

## [SLIDE 13: Pro Tips for Success]
"Tip one: Match the model to your task. Flash for speed, Standard for depth.

Tip two: Rich descriptions work better than vague requests.

Tip three: Be specific about what you want back.

Tip four: Provide all relevant context upfront.

Tip five: Use sequential steps for complex tasks."

## [SLIDE 14: The Template]
"Here's your template for any Gemini task:

First: Define Gemini's persona and expertise.
Second: State your specific task.
Third: Provide complete context and background.
Fourth: Specify the exact output format.

Use this every time, and you'll get great results."

## [SLIDE 15: Final Thought]
"That's the complete guide to prompting Google Gemini in 2026. Remember: Different models, different styles. Use the four-part framework. Be specific and descriptive. Leverage multimodal capabilities.

You're ready to master Gemini prompting. Start implementing these strategies today."
