# Claude Prompting Guide - Narration Script (2-3 minutes)

## [SLIDE 1: Title Slide]
"Welcome to The Complete Guide to Prompting Claude in 2026. I'm going to show you the exact framework that transforms good prompts into exceptional ones."

## [SLIDE 2: The Core Principle]
"Here's the truth about Claude: The difference between a weak prompt and a powerful one isn't about being clever. It's about structure and clarity. Structure is what matters most."

## [SLIDE 3: The 5 Essential Components]
"Every great Claude prompt has five essential components. 

First: Give Claude a clear role or persona. Tell it what expertise to adopt.

Second: State your task explicitly. Be specific about exactly what you want.

Third: Provide detailed context. The more background information, the better the results.

Fourth: Specify your output format. Tell Claude exactly how you want the response structured.

Fifth: Define your success criteria. What makes a response successful for you?"

## [SLIDE 4: Quick Example]
"Here's a real example. Instead of 'write something about marketing,' you'd say: 'You are a professional copywriter. Write a 200-word social media post encouraging newsletter signups. Target audience: entrepreneurs ages 25-45. Tone: professional but conversational. Must include a call-to-action and avoid buzzwords.'

See the difference? Specificity wins."

## [SLIDE 5: Advanced Strategy - XML Tags]
"Claude natively understands XML tags. You can use them to organize complex information. This helps Claude understand the structure of what you're asking."

## [SLIDE 6: Advanced Strategy - Sequential Prompting]
"For complex tasks, break them into 2 to 3 sequential prompts. Don't ask Claude to analyze, summarize, brainstorm, and provide recommendations all in one prompt. It splits its attention.

Instead: First prompt for analysis. Second prompt to build on those results. Third prompt to refine. This approach gets better outputs."

## [SLIDE 7: The Art of Constraints]
"Tell Claude what NOT to do. Strong constraints are as valuable as strong instructions.

Don't use: jargon, passive voice, buzzwords.
Don't include: company politics, unverified claims.
Do include: specific numbers, before-and-after examples, a clear next step.

Negative guidance is surprisingly powerful."

## [SLIDE 8: Common Mistakes]
"Mistake one: Vague prompts. 'Write something about marketing' doesn't work. Be specific.

Mistake two: Too many things at once. You split Claude's focus.

Mistake three: No format specified. Claude guesses and gets it wrong.

Mistake four: Missing context. Claude can't read your mind.

Mistake five: Not showing examples. Examples are worth a hundred words of explanation."

## [SLIDE 9: The Template]
"Here's your template for any Claude task:

One: Your role and expertise.
Two: The specific task.
Three: Relevant background context.
Four: Exactly how to format the response.
Five: What makes it successful.

That's it. Use this formula every time."

## [SLIDE 10: Pro Tips]
"Pro tip one: Break complex tasks into sequential prompts.

Pro tip two: Use XML tags to organize information.

Pro tip three: Show examples of what you want.

Pro tip four: Set clear constraints on what NOT to do.

Pro tip five: Test your best prompts and save them for reuse."

## [SLIDE 11: Final Thought]
"Remember: Structure beats cleverness every single time. Clear, organized prompts beat creative but vague ones.

Your success with Claude comes down to one thing: How well you structure your requests.

That's the complete guide to prompting Claude in 2026. Master these five components, and you'll get exceptional results every time."
